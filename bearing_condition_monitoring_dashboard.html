Predictive Maintenance

AI-generated maintenance recommendations based on bearing health

Immediate: Replace bearing bush
Carbon/PTFE bush severely worn (pressure drop 75%). Transition to PTFE or PEEK-coated SS bush. Bearing cannot safely sustain operation above 600 rpm. Schedule replacement within next maintenance window — estimated ~14h of remaining life.
Urgent: Correct shaft misalignment
38% pressure differential between Plane 1 (1304 kPa) and Plane 2 (832 kPa) confirms misalignment. Re-assemble bearing with precision alignment jigs. Verify concentricity using LVDT before restarting. Post-assembly: confirm symmetric pressure profile (Δ < 5%).
Short-term: Upgrade to pocket-entry design
Current 24-hole orifice entry causes localized jetting and bush erosion. Transition to recessed pocket-entry bearing to distribute lubricant film uniformly, reduce starvation zones, and eliminate cavitation-induced wear at high speeds.
Medium-term: Evaluate water additives
Pure distilled water shows low viscosity limitations (0.65 cSt vs VG2 at 2.2 cSt). Introduce anti-corrosion agents, surfactants, and viscosity-enhancing additives to improve film stability, reduce cavitation risk, and protect SS304 surfaces from water corrosion.
Long-term: Advanced surface coatings
Apply PEEK coating on SS304 bearing liner — similar tribological properties to carbon bush with higher durability. Consider DLC (diamond-like carbon) or graphene-oxide coatings for ultra-low friction in water-lubricated environments per Kim et al. (2015).
